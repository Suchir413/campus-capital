About Suchir

I’m Suchir Padarthi, a senior at Rick Reedy High School in Frisco, Texas and the founder of Campus Capital. My fascination with markets began during a conversation with my dad about market psychology – the idea that markets aren’t just math, they’re collective human behaviour made visible. Since then, I’ve worked as a market analyst intern at Q‑Data, producing bond and equity reports; completed a VC analyst internship evaluating early‑stage startups; founded DIGI‑LEAP to teach Python and machine learning; and studied behavioural economics and technical analysis obsessively. Campus Capital started as a way to organise my own research. When friends and classmates started asking for my notes, I realised there was a gap: most financial analysis is either too technical for beginners or too simplified to be useful. I created Campus Capital to bridge that gap.

Why I Started Campus Capital

Most financial literacy content for students is either too basic (for example, “What is a stock?”) or too advanced (PhD‑level econometrics), and much of it sits behind paywalls. Campus Capital exists to provide high‑level market analysis that’s accessible to anyone with an internet connection. I use Python to scrape market data and generate visualisations, ensuring every claim is backed by data, not speculation.

My Philosophy

Markets are unforgiving. They reward execution and discipline and punish recklessness. But they’re also predictable – not in their day‑to‑day moves, but in their patterns. Human psychology drives markets, and human psychology hasn’t changed in centuries. That’s what I write about in Campus Capital.
